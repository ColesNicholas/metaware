Title
A demonstration of nonlinear demand characteristics in the 'Mozart Effect' experimental paradigm (Study 2)


Developed by
MW


Checked by
NAC


Details
Mixed Student/non-students
No Payment
Not online


Non-control demand levels
Positive: listening to Mozart’s music will make you physically stronger
Negative: listening to Mozart’s music should cause a decrease in physical strength


Procedure
Play mozart music


DVs
Pushups: number of pushups completed in thirty seconds


Vignette
#35_p_pus: Positive - pushups
Imagine that you are completing an in-person study as a volunteer or for course credit.
The researcher informs you that, based on a growing amount of research, they believe that listening to Mozart’s music will increase your physical strength.
The experimenter plays Mozart music for ten minutes, shuts it off, and then asks you to complete as many pushups as possible in thirty seconds.
#35_n_pus: Negative - pushups
Imagine that you are completing an in-person study as a volunteer or for course credit.
The researcher informs you that, based on a growing amount of research, they believe that listening to Mozart’s music will will decrease your physical strength.
The experimenter plays Mozart music for ten minutes, shuts it off, and then asks you to complete as many pushups as possible in thirty seconds.
